## AI Interview Notes

- **Starts with a friendly hello** - it is checking and calibrating your microphone volume. Once you are clear with that part, it'll move on to the prompt: "Tell me about yourself".
- **"Tell me about yourself"** - the AI doesn't want a generic summary of your life. It will base and tie it on your CV that you uploaded. The strategy here is to be **strictly consistent** - no random fluff.
- **You must know every single detail of your resume**
- **Prepare for highly technical questions**
- **Structure with Clear Logic (STAR Method)** - Because the AI translates your speech to text, it looks for logical keywords and clear problem-solving frameworks. Structure your technical scenario and behavioral questions using the STAR framework: Situation, Task, Action, and Result. Emphasize metrics and distinct technical tools.
- **Keep Answers Concise but Loaded** - Avoid rambling with generic buzzwords. Zara scores your "chain of thought." State your technical approach, explain why you chose it, and conclude cleanly. A forgotten single-text keyword will not get you in trouble.
- **Avoid the "Short Silence" Trap** - Zara is notoriously sensitive to silence and can assume you are done speaking if you pause for too long, cutting you off mid-answer.
  - _The Fix:_ - If you need time to think through a complex problem, explicitly declare it or narrate your thought process out loud (e.g., "Let me think about the data flow for a second... first, I would look at..."). Some candidates successfully pause for 15+ seconds upfront to map out their entire answer before speaking.
  - _The Pivot:_ - If you need a moment, you can also ask Zara to repeat the question to buy yourself processing time. You can also ask to rephrase the question.
